**Complete and Final DJ Library Management Guide**, designed as a single, printable document. It uses a 7-Pillar system and an energy-tagging strategy to ensure every track is instantly searchable and usable during a live set. 

------

**The 7-Pillar DJ Library Architecture**

This system eliminates genre clutter by grouping all music into 7 functional "Super-Genres," based on rhythm, energy, and usage during a gig.

**I. The 7 Main Genre Pillars**

Use these as your primary Genre tag or Playlist name in your DJ software.

| **Genre Tag** | **Description**                                | **Typical BPM Range**     | **Includes (Sub-Genres to Collapse)**                      |
| :------------ | :--------------------------------------------- | :------------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------------- |
| **HOUSE**     | Steady, danceable 4/4 beat; consistent groove. | 115 – 130                 | Deep, Tech, Progressive, Disco, Funky, Jackin', Nu-Disco   |
| **TECHNO**    | Driving, dark, or mechanical 4/4 rhythm.       | 125 – 145+                | Minimal, Acid, Industrial, Driving, Peak-Time, Hard Techno |
| **BASS**      | Broken beats, heavy sub-bass, high energy.     | Variable (mostly 130–175) | Drum & Bass, Dubstep, UK Garage, Breakbeat, Electro        |
| **URBAN**     | Syncopated, swinging rhythm, vocal-forward.    | 70 – 110                  | Hip-Hop, R&B, Afrobeats, Dancehall, Reggaeton, Trap        |
| **POP**       | High-production, catchy hooks, mass appeal.    | Variable                  | Synth-Pop, Dance-Pop, Hyperpop, Chart Hits, 80s New Wave   |
| **ROCK**      | Organic drums, guitars, "live" feel.           | Variable                  | Indie, Alternative, Punk, Classic Rock, Metal              |
| **LEFTFIELD** | Experimental, ambient, atmospheric, or tools.  | Any                       | Ambient, Downtempo, Trip-Hop, Jazz, Intros/Outros, Glitch  |

------

**II. The "Fence-Sitter" Resolution Logic**

If a track fits multiple categories, prioritize how you **functionally use it** in a mix:

- **Rule 1: The "Singalong" Rule:** If the crowd knows the words from the radio, tag it **POP**(even if it has a House beat).
- **Rule 2: The "Beat-Match" Rule:** If you’ll mix it using standard beat-matching, put it in **HOUSE** or **TECHNO**. If you’ll use a broken-beat transition, it's **URBAN** or **BASS**.
- **Rule 3: The "Atmosphere" Rule:** If it's too slow or strange for the main dance floor, it's a **LEFTFIELD** tool.

------

**III. The Smart-Tagging Energy System**

Use the **Comments** or **Tags** field in your DJ software to add a simple 1-letter energy code. This allows you to filter within your 7 main genres instantly.

| **Tag** | **Energy Level** | **When to Use**                                              |
| :------ | :--------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **[L]** | Low              | Warm-up, background, cocktail hour.                          |
| **[M]** | Mid              | Building the night, transitioning genres.                    |
| **[H]** | High             | Peak-time bangers, hands-in-the-air moments.                 |
| **[T]** | Tool             | Loops, acapellas, FX, or purely rhythmic tracks for layering. |

**Example Usage:**

- You are in your **HOUSE** playlist at peak time. You filter the Comments column for **[H]**and instantly see only your high-energy House tracks.
- A classic rock song with a big guitar solo is **ROCK [H]**.
- An ambient house loop is **LEFTFIELD [T]**.

------

**IV. Implementation Protocol**

1. **Backup Your Library:** Always save a copy before mass-editing metadata.
2. **Mass Rename Genres:** Select all tracks in your old "Deep House" folder, change the genre tag to `HOUSE`. Repeat for all old sub-genres.
3. **Apply Energy Tags:** Listen to your tracks and add the appropriate `[L]`, `[M]`, `[H]`, or `[T]` tag to the Comments field.
4. **Delete the Clutter:** Commit to never creating a new genre tag that isn't one of the 7 Pillars.
5. **Order Your Playlists:** Name your playlists `01_HOUSE`, `02_TECHNO`, `03_BASS`, etc., so they appear in a consistent, energy-building order in your software.
